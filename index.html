Projects

Post-Stroke Rehabilitation Device

Led a team of undergraduate students to develop a wearable post-stroke rehabilitation device, designed to alleviate wrist and hand contractures that hinder finger movement post-stroke. This innovative solution, featuring mechanical, electrical, and software elements, assists users in achieving repetitive finger flexion and extension while prioritizing safety and comfort, ultimately promoting effective rehabilitation.

Wearable Sleep Apnea Monitoring Device

Collaborated with a team of undergraduate students to develop a pioneering wearable device aimed at diagnosing obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) among the general population. This innovative solution integrates a pulse oximeter sensor within a user-friendly finger brace, continuously monitoring heart rate and SpO2 levels during sleep. By intelligently detecting critical periods and performing in-depth time domain analysis, the device generates comprehensive sleep reports, potentially revolutionizing OSA diagnostics and addressing the challenge of undiagnosed cases.

Physiological Model for Optimal Soccer Penalty Kick

Collaborated with a team to develop a physiological model focused on penalty kicks in soccer. This project utilized a simplified 2D biomechanical system to optimize foot velocity while minimizing the risk of quadriceps muscle injuries. Examined various factors through rigorous Python simulations, including initial conditions, muscle force, and activation patterns, ultimately identifying key parameters and the limiting muscle responsible for injury prevention. Research sheds new light on the biomechanics of penalty kicks, with practical implications for soccer performance and player safety.

NICU Incubator System Design

Designed and developed a sophisticated circuit to replicate the critical functions of a Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) incubator, utilizing Arduino and a range of components including temperature sensors and LEDs. The innovative software implementation featured precise timers and interrupt-driven alerts, ensuring the monitoring and control of incubator conditions, with built-in critical condition detection for enhanced patient safety.

Cochlear Implant Signal Processing Algorithm

Collaborated within a team of engineers to develop a cochlear implant signal processing algorithm in MATLAB. Leveraged deep knowledge of linear signals and systems to design precision filters for sound signal processing, achieving remarkable results such as a 95% user satisfaction rating through rigorous evaluations of signal noise, latency, and word intelligibility.

QRS Complex Classification for ECG Signals

Developed a Python-based QRS complex characterization algorithm for ECG signals, enabling precise detection of cardiac events and offering valuable insights into heart rate analysis. The project encompassed signal preprocessing, complex pattern recognition, and advanced data visualization techniques.
